--- Q&A ITEM ---
question: My dryer runs but produces no heat — clothes come out still damp. How do I fix this?

answer: |
  Tools needed: Phillips screwdriver, multimeter, replacement thermal fuse (under $10 at any
  appliance parts store — search your model number for the exact part).

  1. Unplug the dryer from the wall outlet before any disassembly.
  2. Pull the dryer away from the wall and remove the back panel (6–8 Phillips screws).
  3. Locate the thermal fuse — it is a small white or black plastic component mounted
     directly on the exhaust duct near the heating element. It has two wire connectors.
  4. Set your multimeter to continuity mode and probe both terminals of the thermal fuse.
     A blown fuse reads no continuity (open circuit). A working fuse beeps or reads near 0 Ω.
  5. If blown: pull off the two wire connectors, unscrew the fuse (one screw), install the
     new fuse, reconnect wires. Do not overtighten — the housing is plastic.
  6. BEFORE reassembling, disconnect and clean the entire exhaust duct from dryer to wall
     exit with a dryer vent brush kit. A clogged vent is what blew the fuse — skipping
     this step means the new fuse will blow again within weeks.
  7. Reinstall the back panel, plug in, run a test cycle with a damp towel.

equipment_problem: Dryer runs but produces no heat

tools_required:
  - Phillips screwdriver
  - multimeter
  - replacement thermal fuse (model-specific)
  - dryer vent brush kit

steps:
  - Unplug dryer from wall outlet
  - Remove back panel screws and pull panel off
  - Locate the thermal fuse on the exhaust duct near the heating element
  - Test fuse continuity with multimeter — no continuity confirms it is blown
  - Replace blown fuse with correct part number; reconnect wire terminals
  - Clean full exhaust duct with vent brush kit before reassembly
  - Reinstall panel, plug in, run test cycle

safety_info: |
  Unplug the dryer from its 240V outlet before removing the back panel. Unlike a standard
  120V outlet, a dryer outlet carries enough current to cause fatal electrocution on
  contact — do not rely on the circuit breaker alone; physically pull the plug.

tips:
  - Remember to clean the lint trap regularly.
  - If the dryer still does not heat after replacing the thermal fuse, call an appliance
    repair technician.
