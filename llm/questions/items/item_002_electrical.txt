--- Q&A ITEM ---
question: The outlet in my kitchen stopped working. How do I replace it?

answer: |
  Tools: flathead screwdriver, Phillips screwdriver, non-contact voltage tester, needle-nose
  pliers, electrical tape, replacement 15A or 20A outlet (match the amperage on the old one).

  1. Go to your breaker panel and flip the breaker for the kitchen circuit to OFF.
  2. Remove the outlet cover plate (one center screw) and unscrew the outlet from the box
     (two screws top and bottom).
  3. Gently pull the outlet out 3–4 inches to expose the wires. Do NOT touch the wires yet.
  4. Replace the outlet: connect black wire to the brass screw (hot), white wire to the
     silver screw (neutral), bare copper or green wire to the green screw (ground).
     Tighten each screw firmly — a loose connection causes arcing.
  5. Push the outlet back into the box, screw it in, replace the cover plate.
  6. Restore the breaker and test with a lamp or phone charger.

equipment_problem: Kitchen outlet stopped working

tools_required:
  - flathead screwdriver
  - Phillips screwdriver
  - non-contact voltage tester
  - needle-nose pliers
  - electrical tape
  - replacement outlet (15A or 20A)

steps:
  - Flip the correct circuit breaker to OFF at the panel
  - Remove the cover plate and unscrew the outlet from the electrical box
  - Pull the outlet out and use the voltage tester to confirm no live power
  - Disconnect old outlet; connect wires to new outlet matching terminal colors
  - Push outlet back in, screw down, replace cover plate
  - Restore breaker and test the outlet

safety_info: Be careful when working with electricity. Make sure to turn things off first.

tips:
  - Take your time and double-check your work.
  - If you are not confident, call a licensed electrician.
